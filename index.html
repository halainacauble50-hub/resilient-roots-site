About the Book

Resilient Roots is a powerful memoir about survival, faith, family, and the courage to begin again. From childhood in Ramallah to rebuilding life in the United States, Halaina Cauble tells a story of loss, resilience, and the quiet strength it takes to reclaim your life.

Written with honesty, warmth, and hard-won clarity, this is a story for readers who believe broken ground can still grow something beautiful.

About the Author

Halaina Cauble

Halaina Cauble is the author of Resilient Roots, a literary memoir tracing her journey from survival to self-reclamation. A mother of four, entrepreneur, and salon owner, she rebuilt her life after abuse, betrayal, financial loss, and displacement—emerging not hardened, but grounded.

She lives in South Carolina, where she continues to write, travel, and build spaces rooted in connection and strength.

Opening Ground

A boy pressed his ear to the door.

“How are we supposed to feed another mouth?”

Inside, Yamma measured water into a chipped cup, careful not to spill. She tipped the rice tin and shook it once, then again, listening for what remained.

I was born into a house that already knew how to be careful — food rationed, space shared. Nothing was wasted. Not water. Not bread. Not movement.
