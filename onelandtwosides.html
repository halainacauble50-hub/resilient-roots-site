One Land, Two Sides explores the history of Israel and Palestine through the voices, events, and experiences that shaped the region. Rather than arguing for one side, the book seeks understanding through historical context and personal connection.

From the Ottoman era to the British Mandate, from the creation of Israel to the present day, the book traces the major events that continue to influence the region. Historical facts are combined with personal reflections and family memories from Ramallah.

This book was written to provide context, understanding, and perspective. It recognizes that history is often experienced differently depending on where a person stands, and that human stories exist behind every headline and statistic.

Excerpt

History is often told as dates, treaties, and wars. But history is also kitchens. Courtyards. Family stories repeated so often they become part of who we are. Before there were borders and checkpoints, there were ordinary lives unfolding across the hills and valleys of Palestine. People planted olive trees. Raised children. Built homes. Planned futures. The story begins there.
